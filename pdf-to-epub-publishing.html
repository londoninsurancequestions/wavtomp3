Where you'll upload

Platform guide — what each store expects.

Every major ebook portal accepts EPUB for reflowable books. Requirements differ slightly — pick the matching preset above, then follow each portal’s cover and metadata steps.

Amazon KDP

EPUB

Kindle Direct Publishing converts your EPUB to Kindle format on ingest. Fiction and nonfiction almost always need reflowable text with a working TOC.

  • Use the KDP preset — Kindle output profile, page numbers stripped
  • Upload cover JPEG separately in the KDP dashboard
  • Preview in Kindle Previewer before publishing
kdp.amazon.com →

Apple Books

EPUB

Submit via Apple Books for Authors or an aggregator. Apple’s validator is strict about EPUB structure and embedded metadata.

  • Use the Apple Books preset — fill title and author before converting
  • Cover uploaded separately (minimum 1400px on shortest side)
  • Run through Apple’s Books upload validator if a reject occurs
authors.apple.com →

Kobo Writing Life

EPUB

Kobo’s direct author portal accepts EPUB and distributes to Kobo ereaders and apps worldwide.

  • Use the Kobo WL preset for Kobo-tuned CSS
  • EPUB 3 reflow recommended for new titles
  • ISBN optional for Kobo-exclusive titles in many regions
kobo.com/writinglife →

Google Play Books

EPUB

Google Play Books Partner Center accepts EPUB for most genres. Reflowable interiors work across Android phones and tablets.

  • Use the Google Play preset — embed fonts for consistent typography
  • Cover and description entered in Play Books dashboard
  • Google may run additional processing after upload
play.google.com/books/publish →

Before you hit publish

Pre-upload checklist.

Five minutes that save a rejection email

  1. Start from a text PDF — export from Word or InDesign with real text, not a flat scan. OCR first if needed.
  2. Set title and author in the converter so embedded metadata matches your store listing.
  3. Spot-check chapters — open the EPUB in Apple Books or Kindle Previewer and click through the TOC.
  4. Prepare your cover separately — JPG or PNG at the resolution each portal specifies.
  5. Proof on a phone — reflow, font size, and paragraph spacing look different on a 6″ screen than on your monitor.

Publisher FAQ

Common questions before upload.

Is EPUB what KDP actually wants?+
Yes. KDP accepts EPUB (recommended), DOCX, and other formats. EPUB gives you the most predictable reflow on Kindle devices. Amazon converts it to KPF/AZW on their side — you don’t need to deliver MOBI anymore.
Should I upload the same EPUB everywhere?+
Often yes — a clean universal EPUB works on Draft2Digital and IngramSpark. For direct uploads, store-specific presets tweak CSS profiles so typography renders better on each ecosystem. Content is the same; packaging differs slightly.
Where does the cover go?+
Every platform uploads cover art separately in their dashboard. This tool converts the interior manuscript only. Export your cover as JPG or PNG at the size the store specifies.
Do I need an ISBN for the ebook?+
Depends on the store and your goals. KDP assigns a free ASIN for Kindle-only titles. Apple, IngramSpark, and wide distribution usually need an ISBN. Draft2Digital can provide one in some cases. The EPUB itself doesn’t require an ISBN to be generated here.
My PDF is from a printed book scan — will it work?+
Only if the PDF contains searchable text (OCR). Image-only scans produce a poor EPUB. Run OCR first, then convert. For best results, always export from the original Word or InDesign source.
Does this add DRM?+
No. We output a standard unencrypted EPUB. Stores may optionally wrap their own DRM at publish time (e.g. Kindle DRM) based on your account settings — that happens on their servers, not here.
